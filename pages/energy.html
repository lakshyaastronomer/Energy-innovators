BIOMASS ENERGY
RENEWABLE
Biomass energy comes from organic materials like wood, agricultural crops, or waste. It can be burned directly for heat, or converted into biofuels for electricity and transport. Biomass helps reduce reliance on fossil fuels, though sustainability depends on responsible sourcing and reforestation.
HYDROPOWER
RENEWABLE
Hydropower captures energy from flowing water—rivers, dams, or tides—to generate electricity. It’s one of the oldest renewable sources and can provide reliable, large-scale power. However, large dams can affect ecosystems and communities if not carefully planned.
SOLAR ENERGY
RENEWABLE
Solar power harnesses sunlight with photovoltaic cells or thermal systems. It is clean, abundant, and scalable from rooftop panels to massive solar farms. Advances in storage and efficiency continue to make solar one of the fastest-growing renewable technologies worldwide.
GEOTHERMAL ENERGY
RENEWABLE
Geothermal energy taps heat stored beneath the Earth’s crust. Wells bring steam or hot water to the surface to generate electricity or provide direct heating. It is a reliable source of baseload power but limited to regions with suitable geological conditions.
WIND ENERGY
RENEWABLE
Wind energy uses turbines powered by moving air to generate electricity. It is clean, cost-effective, and scalable, from small community turbines to large offshore wind farms. Integration with the grid and environmental considerations are key factors for growth.
